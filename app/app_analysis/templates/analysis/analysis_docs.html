4.4 Effect of Process Digitization on Productivity (RQ1)

Addresses Questions Q14-Q24

Descriptive & Interpretive Analysis

Analyze core digitization features (Q20-Q23), productivity measurement (Q14-Q16), and the outcome (Q24).

"While respondents strongly agree that order tracking is done in the system (Q21, M=4.5), they show less agreement that they are awarded based on performance (Q17, M=2.8). This suggests a disconnect between the digital tracking of productivity and its use in employee incentive structures."

Inferential Analysis (Postgraduate Level)

Analysis Steps
  1. Composite Score: Create a new variable, Digitization_Score, by averaging scores of Q19, Q20, Q21, Q22, Q23.
  2. Correlation Analysis: Run a Pearson correlation between Digitization_Score and Q24 (Meeting production targets).
  3. ANOVA: Run a one-way ANOVA to see if the Digitization_Score differs by Q1 (Role).

Expected Finding & Report Sample:

"To determine the relationship between process digitization and productivity, a Pearson correlation was computed. The results showed a strong, positive, and statistically significant correlation between the Digitization Score and the frequency of meeting production targets (r = .55, p < .001), indicating that as the level of process digitization increases, so does the ability to meet production targets."

4.5 Effect of Process Standardization on Productivity (RQ2)

Addresses Questions Q25-Q34

Descriptive & Interpretive Analysis

Focus on benefits (Q25, Q26, Q29, Q34) and challenges (Q31, Q32, Q33), looking for nuanced findings.

"The findings present a compelling duality. While respondents strongly agree that PLM-driven standardization improves production accuracy (Q34, M=4.4), they also strongly agree that processes and styles vary significantly between buyers (Q31 & Q33). This suggests a key challenge is not a lack of internal standardization, but managing multiple, conflicting external standards imposed by buyers."

Inferential Analysis

Analysis Steps
  1. Composite Scores: Create Standardization_Benefits (avg of Q25, Q26, Q29, Q34) and Standardization_Challenges (avg of Q31, Q32, Q33).
  2. Correlation Analysis: Correlate Standardization_Benefits with Q34. Then, correlate Standardization_Challenges with Standardization_Benefits to find potential negative relationships.
  3. T-test: Compare the mean score for Standardization_Challenges between companies that serve many buyers (Q7) vs. few.

4.6 Effect of Process Automation on Productivity (RQ3)

Addresses Questions Q35-Q44

Descriptive & Interpretive Analysis

Contrast machine prevalence (Q35-Q37) with digital integration (Q41). Analyze human factors (Q38, Q40) and productivity outcomes (Q42, Q43, Q44).

"A low mean on Q41 (Automatic data input) indicates that automation is primarily mechanical, not integrated into the digital data flow—a lower level of maturity."

Inferential Analysis

Analysis Steps
  1. Composite Score: Create an Automation_Outcome score by averaging Q42, Q43, Q44.
  2. Correlation Analysis: Correlate Automation_Outcome with Q38 (Trained operators) and Q41 (Automatic data input).
  3. T-test/ANOVA: Use Q2 (Do you have people reporting to you?) as a grouping variable to compare mean perception of Q44 (Minimal idle time).

Expected Finding: The correlation with Q41 might be stronger, proving integrated automation has a greater impact than just having machines.

4.7 Summary of Findings

Conclude the chapter by synthesizing the key results into a powerful summary table.

Research Question Key Finding Summary
RQ1 (Digitization) Process digitization was found to have a significant positive relationship with meeting production targets.
RQ2 (Standardization) While PLM-driven standardization significantly improves accuracy, its effectiveness is challenged by the varying standards imposed by different international buyers.
RQ3 (Automation) Automation, particularly when integrated with automatic data collection, significantly reduces waste and machine idle time. The availability of trained operators was also a key factor.
